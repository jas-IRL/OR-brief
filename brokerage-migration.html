What They've Been Trained On

All 11 FE Specialists completed brokerage onboarding training in Docebo. Three brokerage-specific courses: Cash Investing Overview, Brokerage Direct & Voicemail Case Handling, and Regulator for Brokerage Ops. This training covers Tier 1 brokerage work — account setup, basic trade inquiries, and standard regulatory workflows.
FE has been trained on Tier 1 brokerage scenarios. No training exists for Tier 2 work because the Tier 2 scope has not been defined. We do not know which knowledge articles or work instructions FE advocates reference day-to-day, or whether those articles cover the workflows CNX was handling. If CNX relied on tribal knowledge not captured in documentation, FE may inherit work without the guidance they need.
What We Don't Have Visibility Into
Contact type distribution. We know the total volume but not how it breaks down by scenario type (account issues, trade problems, regulatory inquiries, etc.). This matters for scoping Tier 2.
Tier 1 vs Tier 2 volume split. We don't know how much of the current work is Tier 1 (what FE is trained for) versus Tier 2 (what may shift from internal).
Escalation patterns. We see 1–4 cases/week moving to Manager Escalations, but we don't know what triggers those escalations or whether FE has clear rules on when to escalate.
Tool permissions. We have not confirmed whether FE has the same access as internal advocates in CF1, Copilot, Regulator, and Toolbox. Permission gaps on FINRA-regulated work are a compliance risk.
Knowledge article coverage. We don't know which articles exist for brokerage workflows, whether they cover CNX's work, or whether FE advocates can find and use them effectively.
Hours of operation. We have not confirmed FE's brokerage coverage hours or whether they will change post-migration. HOOP gaps create service risk.

Confounder Scan

When we measure whether this migration is working, other things happening at the same time could move the same metrics and make it hard to tell what caused the change. These are the variables we need to account for. If a metric shifts after launch, check these before attributing it to the migration.

Tax Season (Feb–Apr)
Volume spikes may mask or amplify performance shifts during the migration window.
FE Tenure Ramp
120-day advocates improving naturally — this will confound migration impact measurement.
Internal Restructuring
Redeployment could shift internal performance independently of migration.
QA Rubric Changes
Any scoring changes would confound quality comparisons against pre-migration baseline.
Cash Investing Product Changes
Feature launches could shift contact types and skew volume distribution.
