PMD Disputes Platform
Migration

Paper Money Deposit (PMD) claims are migrating from manual QuickText-driven intake to the structured Disputes platform to meet Regulation E requirements for Electronic Funds Transfers. Frontline advocates will move to a guided Toolbox "Submit Dispute" experience with structured error reasons and automatic routing. Launch is March 27, 2026 with a hard regulatory deadline of March 31, 2026.

What's Changing
PMD dispute intake is moving from manual QuickText-driven workflows to the structured Disputes platform. Today, advocates gather Notice of Error information using PMD QuickTexts (NMI, NMI Incomplete, Unable to Locate – No Barcode) and manually transfer cases to the Dispute – Paper Money queue. This creates inconsistent data capture, manual routing errors, and limited auditability.

After March 31, advocates will select "Submit Dispute" in Toolbox and complete a guided questionnaire with required fields. The new flow includes: disclosure acknowledgment, 6 structured error reason categories (Incorrect Amount, Wrong Fees, Funds Unavailable, Unauthorized, Missing from Statement, Deposit Didn't Show), store confirmation gate that blocks disputes if the store didn't collect funds, optional evidence upload, and transaction lookup support. Routing becomes automatic based on error reason and transaction data.

All frontline advocates across Voice, Messaging, and Email who handle PMD disputes will experience the new Toolbox intake flow. The Disputes team continues to own investigation and resolution. Existing Reg E investigation standards and SLAs remain unchanged. Ownership model does not change — only the intake mechanism.
Regulatory Context
Regulation E Compliance
PMD disputes qualify as Electronic Funds Transfers under Regulation E. The regulation requires consistent documentation, structured Notice of Error capture, and auditability. The current manual QT-driven process creates compliance risk through inconsistent data capture and manual routing. This migration moves intake into a standardized framework that meets Reg E requirements and supports MAP consent order commitments.

Advocate testing results. No advocate testing before production. First exposure to new flow will be in live customer interactions. Job aids and post-launch monitoring mitigate this risk.
Toolbox error rate baseline. We don't know how often advocates encounter technical errors in Toolbox flows. This matters for distinguishing between advocate confusion and system issues.
